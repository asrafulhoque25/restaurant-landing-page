7 ways your current website is costing you covers

If three or more of these sound familiar, you're losing customers before they even pick up the phone.

1

Your menu is a PDF

Customers have to pinch and zoom on their phone. Google can't read it for search. You can't update prices without re-uploading. PDFs lose you customers and rankings — both.

2

DoorDash & Uber Eats are eating profit

15-30% commission on every order. No customer data. No way to bring those diners back without paying again. A direct-ordering site keeps that money in your pocket.

3

No reservations from your website

Diners want to book a table at 10pm Tuesday for Saturday's anniversary. If they have to call during your busiest service window, they'll just book a competitor with online reservations.

4

Your food photos look amateur

45% of diners look at food photos first. Bad photos signal bad food. Even if your kitchen is incredible, low-quality photos cost you the click. Layout matters as much as the camera.

5

You're invisible on Google Maps

42% of local restaurant searchers click results in the Map 3-pack. If you're not in those three, you're competing for scraps. The sites that win combine on-page SEO with a fully optimized Google Business Profile.

6

No catering or private events page

Catering orders are 5-10x larger than dine-in tickets. Private events fill slow weekday nights. If neither has a dedicated page, you're missing the highest-margin revenue your restaurant could be making.

7

Mobile diners can't find your phone number, hours, or directions in 5 seconds

72% of restaurant searches happen on phones. People standing on a street corner, walking from a meeting, or driving in their car. If your hours are buried, your phone number is hidden, or your address requires a hunt, they'll close the tab and try the next listing in 5 seconds flat.

Pages that turn each revenue stream into a real business

Most restaurants are leaving money on the table because their website only sells dine-in.

Dine-in & reservations

Your homepage anchor. Big food photos, online booking, hours, directions, parking info. The first impression that decides whether someone walks through your door tonight.

Online ordering & pickup

Direct ordering page that bypasses DoorDash. Mobile-first menu with category tabs, photos, and modifiers. Connects to your POS so orders print in the kitchen automatically.

Catering

Office lunches, weddings, large parties. Dedicated page with package pricing, lead time, delivery radius, and a contact form that captures orders 5-10x bigger than your average dine-in ticket.

Private events & buyouts

Birthday parties, anniversaries, holiday work parties. Photos of your private dining room, capacity, packages, and pricing. Fills slow Sunday-Tuesday nights with high-margin events.

Gift cards

Digital gift cards customers buy in 30 seconds and email to a recipient. Spike sales every November-December. Most independent restaurants skip this and lose thousands in holiday revenue.

Specials & events page

Live music nights, taco Tuesday, prix fixe Sundays. A regularly updated section that gives Google fresh content (great for SEO) and gives diners a reason to come back week after week.

Food first design

45% of restaurant website visitors look at food photos first. We design every layout around making your food look as good on screen as it does on the plate.

Hero photography that travels

Full-bleed hero images of your signature dishes. Compressed for fast mobile loads (under 200KB) but visually rich. The first thing visitors see, and the thing that decides whether they keep scrolling.

Menu photos for the dishes that matter

Not every dish needs a photo. We help you identify the 6-12 dishes that drive the majority of orders, then design the menu so those photos do the heavy lifting. Restaurants with photos see 30-40% higher conversion.

Vibe photos and lifestyle shots

The dining room. Happy diners. Bartender pouring a cocktail. These shots sell the experience, not just the food. Especially important for special-occasion dining and private events.

Photo brief if you don't have shots yet

Don't have professional food photos? We give you a shot-list brief covering exactly what to capture, how to light it, and what angles work. Many restaurants get great results with a smartphone and 30 minutes of natural light.

Questions restaurant owners actually ask

Will the new site work with my existing POS and reservation system? +

Yes. We integrate with all major POS systems including Toast, Square, Clover, and Lightspeed — as well as reservation platforms like OpenTable, Resy, and Tock. We'll confirm compatibility during our scoping call before any work begins.

I don't have professional food photos. Is that a problem? +

Not at all. We provide a detailed photo brief covering exactly what to shoot, how to light it, and which angles work best. Many of our clients get great results with just a smartphone and 30 minutes of natural light.

Will direct ordering really beat DoorDash and Uber Eats? +

On margin, absolutely. DoorDash and Uber Eats take 15–30% per order. Your own ordering system charges a flat monthly fee. Once you hit 75 direct orders, the site pays for itself — and every order after that is pure margin recovery.

How long until I see results? +

The site launches in 14 days. Direct orders typically start within the first week. SEO results build over 60–90 days. Google Business Profile improvements are often visible within 2–3 weeks of setup.

What happens if I want to update my menu seasonally? +

We train your team to make menu and price updates themselves — no developer needed. If you'd rather hand it off, we offer affordable update packages. You own the site outright, so you're never locked in.

Will my Google rankings transfer if I rebuild? +

Yes, we handle full SEO migration — 301 redirects, URL structure preservation, and meta data transfer. Your existing rankings are protected, and the improved on-page SEO typically boosts them within a few months.

Why only $999 when agencies charge $5K–$15K? +

We built a repeatable system specifically for restaurants. No custom discovery, no bloated retainers, no account managers. We pass those savings directly to you. Same quality output, fraction of the overhead.
